How did Robert James Fischer have an impact on the Military?

People in the United States were proud of Fischer's chess victory. His triumph over Boris Spassky in the 1972 World Chess Championship was presented as a metaphor for the triumph of Western individuality over Soviet communism.

As a show of intellectual dominance, the Soviets made significant investments in chess. This had then boosted the economy by people interested in the competition to be one of the most important figures who could defeat Robert James Fischer.

During the Cold War, the story of individualistic innovation—a pillar of Western ideology—reincarnated with his character as a lone genius.

How did Mikhail Tal have an impact on the Military?

His methods were similar to military operations that involved surprise attacks, calculated risks, and unusual tactics; hence, military strategists and analysts could learn a lot from his games.

Tal demonstrated an awareness of mental dynamics by using bold tactics to startle opponents. This was particularly important to military tactics used during the Cold War that aimed at limiting the enemies ability to make decisions.

His exercises created the idea that victory may be achieved by causing uncertainty and taking advantage of psychological vulnerabilities; this idea is relevant to both military and intelligence operations. This also showed that sacrifices can be used as an upperhand sometimes.
